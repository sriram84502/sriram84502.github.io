Encrypted Dynamic RVFLN Ensemble Learning for Privacy-Preserving State-of-Health Prediction in Electric Vehicle Batteries Using Fully Homomorphic Encryption CKKS

                        
S. S. Vanapalli, et al.

                        
                        

                            
                            

                                

                                    Published in Transactions on Emerging Telecommunications Technologies (Wiley, 2026), this paper introduces the EDRVFLN framework. We integrate Dynamic Random Vector Functional Link Networks (RVFLNs) with Ensemble Learning to model complex, nonlinear battery degradation. To guarantee complete data privacy, the Cheon-Kim-Kim-Song (CKKS) Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) scheme is deployed, enabling both model training and predictive inference directly on encrypted floating-point ciphertexts without decryption. Evaluated using the standard NASA lithium-ion battery telemetry datasets, the EDRVFLN system matches the predictive accuracy of traditional unencrypted architectures while ensuring absolute, mathematically proven data confidentiality throughout the analytical pipeline.

EDL-DRVFLNN: Ensemble Deep Learning with Dynamic Random Vector Functional Link Neural Networks for Privacy-Preserving Electric Vehicle Battery Health Prediction

                        
S. S. Vanapalli, et al.

                        
                        

                            
                            

                                

                                    Published in Measurement (Elsevier, 2025), this paper presents EDL-DRVFLNN, a privacy-preserving ensemble deep learning framework utilizing Dynamic Random Vector Functional Link Neural Networks (DRVFLNN) for State-of-Health (SoH) prediction. By incorporating randomized learning mechanisms, the DRVFLNN architecture adapts dynamically to battery aging patterns with rapid closed-form training. Under strict privacy constraints, the system operates on telemetry datasets encrypted via the CKKS homomorphic scheme. Validation on NASA battery degradation profiles demonstrates that EDL-DRVFLNN achieves superior prediction robustness and minimized Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) under full cryptographic privacy compliance.

                            2025
                            Neural Computing and Applications
                        

                        
PPDNN-SoH: Privacy-Preserving Deep Neural Network for Sustainable Electric Vehicle Battery State of Health Prediction

                        
S. S. Vanapalli, et al.

                        
                        

                            
                            

                                

                                    Published in Neural Computing and Applications (Springer, 2025), this paper proposes PPDNN-SoH, a privacy-preserving deep neural network framework for sustainable battery health tracking. The system integrates Homomorphic Encryption (HE) using the TenSEAL package to perform multi-layered neural network feedforward calculations directly on encrypted telemetry. PPDNN-SoH provides comprehensive cryptographic protection across training data, model parameters, and input/output states. Evaluated on NASA lithium-ion battery cell datasets (B0005, B0006, B0007, and B0018), the architecture demonstrates robust defenses against model inversion, extraction, membership inference, evasion, and data poisoning attacks while matching standard DNN accuracy with practical computational overhead.

                            2024
                            Computers & Electrical Engineering
                        

                        
Privacy-Preserving State of Health Prediction for Electric Vehicle Batteries: A Comprehensive Review

                        
S. S. Vanapalli, et al.

                        
                        

                            
                            

                                

                                    Published in Computers & Electrical Engineering (Elsevier, 2024), this comprehensive review examines the intersection of data-driven Battery Management Systems (BMS) and user privacy. As raw telemetry (voltage, temperature, current) poses severe security risks, we systematically analyze state-of-the-art privacy-preserving paradigms in EV diagnostics. The review evaluates cryptographic and decentralized architectures integrating Homomorphic Encryption (HE), Federated Learning (FL), Multi-Party Computation (MPC), and Differential Privacy (DP). By comparing key performance trade-offs in accuracy, communication latency, and security bounds, we establish secure next-generation BMS design paradigms for intelligent transportation systems.
